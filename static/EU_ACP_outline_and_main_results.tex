\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This paper examines whether intra-regional trade integration among African, Caribbean, and
Pacific (ACP) countries acts as a stumbling block or building block for their bilateral trade
with the European Union. Using a panel of 78 ACP countries across seven Regional Economic
Communities (RECs) paired with all 27 EU member states over 1995--2021, we estimate PPML
gravity models with exporter-year, ACP-country, and year fixed effects. Higher intra-REC
trade shares are associated with significantly lower EU-ACP bilateral trade (coefficient
$-0.564$, s.e.\ $0.322$, $p<0.10$), consistent with a stumbling-block effect. At the
sample mean intra-REC trade share of 11.6\%, bilateral trade is approximately 6.3\% lower;
at the 90th percentile (25.5\%), the reduction reaches 13.4\%. The stumbling-block pattern
is robust across all seven RECs and is driven entirely by EU exports to ACP markets rather
than ACP exports to the EU. Economic Partnership Agreements (EPAs) significantly moderate
the stumbling-block effect ($+1.048$, $p<0.05$), suggesting that deeper EU-ACP trade
agreements can partially offset the trade-diverting effects of regional integration.
\end{abstract}

%===========================================================================
\section*{Paper Outline}
%===========================================================================

\begin{enumerate}

  \item \textbf{Introduction}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Research question: does intra-ACP regional integration help or hinder EU-ACP trade?
    \item Stumbling block vs.\ building block debate \`{a} la Bhagwati (1993)
    \item Preview of main findings and contribution
    \item Relevance for EPA design and the post-Cotonou framework
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Institutional Background}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Cotonou Agreement (2000): shift from non-reciprocal Lom\'{e} preferences to EPAs
    \item ACP groupings: ECOWAS, SADC, EAC, COMESA, Central Africa, CARIFORUM, PIF
    \item EPA implementation timeline: CARIFORUM (2008), Pacific Gateway (2009),
          ESA-4 (2012), Cameroon/Fiji (2014), SADC (2016), Mozambique (2018), Comoros (2019)
    \item EAC EPA: initialled but never provisionally applied within the sample period
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Data and Descriptive Statistics}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Trade flows: BACI HS92 V202601 (CEPII), 1995--2021
    \item Gravity variables: CEPII Gravity Dataset V202211 (distance, language, colonial tie)
    \item GDP/population: World Bank WDI; Cook Islands and Niue patched manually
    \item Panel: $78 \times 27 \times 27 = 56{,}862$ potential dyad-years;
          PPML sample 45,647 after conditioning on data availability
    \item Key variable: intra-REC trade share (IT share) $=$ intra-bloc trade / total trade,
          time-varying at the country level
    \item SACU imputation: five SACU members assigned non-SACU SADC annual mean
          for years with missing or zero BACI intra-bloc flows
    \item Summary statistics by REC: Table~\ref{tab:summary_stats}
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Empirical Strategy}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item PPML gravity model \`{a} la Santos Silva and Tenreyro (2006)
    \item Fixed effects: exporter $\times$ year, ACP-country, year
    \item Note on FE structure: importer $\times$ year FEs would absorb IT share and EPA
          (both vary only at the ACP-country $\times$ year level), so these are split into
          ACP-country FEs and year FEs to preserve identification while controlling for
          common time shocks
    \item Identification: within-country variation in IT share over time,
          across 27 EU partner-year observations per country-year
    \item Secondary specification: IT intensity (IT share normalised by the bloc's world
          trade share)
    \item Interaction specification: EPA $\times$ IT share to test EPA moderation
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Main Results}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Column (1): Baseline PPML---distance $-1.729^{***}$, language $+1.107^{***}$
    \item Column (2): IT share $-0.564^{*}$ (s.e.\ $0.322$)---stumbling-block confirmed
    \item Column (3): EPA $\times$ IT share $+1.048^{**}$; EPA attenuates stumbling block;
          sign reversal requires IT share $> 0.60$ (outside sample support)
    \item Column (4): IT intensity $-0.006^{***}$; result robust to integration measure
    \item Column (5): OLS (zeros dropped) $-1.478^{***}$; consistent in sign
    \item Marginal effects at mean (11.6\%): $-6.3\%$ bilateral trade;
          at 90th percentile (25.5\%): $-13.4\%$
    \item Figure~\ref{fig:descriptive}: IT share distribution and time series by REC
    \item Figure~\ref{fig:results}: IT share vs.\ EU-ACP trade scatter by REC
          (negative slopes throughout)
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Mechanisms and Heterogeneity}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Trade direction: stumbling block driven by EU exports to ACP ($-1.027^{***}$),
          not ACP exports to EU ($-0.059$, insignificant)---consistent with import
          substitution displacing European goods
    \item REC subsamples: all seven RECs negative; five of six interpretable estimates
          significant; range $-0.75$ (Central Africa, n.s.) to $-3.02^{***}$ (EAC)
    \item SADC EPA coefficient positive ($+0.408^{***}$): staggered entry generates
          genuine within-REC identification
    \item PIF instability: positive distance coefficient ($+13.87$) flags poorly conditioned
          PPML; excluded from coefficient stability plots
    \item Regional subsamples: Africa $-0.357$ (n.s.); Caribbean $-2.327^{**}$;
          heterogeneity across African blocs attenuates the pooled Africa estimate
    \item Figure~\ref{fig:results}: REC forest plot (coefficients with 95\% CI, PIF excluded)
    \item Figure~\ref{fig:results}: Marginal effects curve with REC mean IT shares indicated
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Robustness Checks}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item NB-PML: coefficient $-0.458$ (s.e.\ $0.360$), $\approx 81\%$ of PPML
          baseline---confirms overdispersion is not biasing PPML standard errors
    \item From 1998: $-1.336^{***}$---early sparse BACI years attenuate the baseline
    \item Excl.\ South Africa: $-0.460$ (n.s.); excl.\ Nigeria: $-0.344$ (n.s.)
          ---dominant traders carry identification weight in the aggregate
    \item Excl.\ SACU: $-1.590^{***}$---imputation attenuates, does not generate, the result
    \item Excl.\ Somalia/Eritrea: $-0.569^{*}$---near-zero trade, no material effect
    \item Excl.\ CARIFORUM: $-0.420$ (n.s.); excl.\ PIF: $-0.506$ (n.s.)---reduced
          cross-bloc variation expected to widen standard errors
    \item OLS $\log(1+y)$ sign reversal: a well-known artefact of the transformation
          near zero; not interpretable
  \end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Conclusion}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Strong and consistent stumbling-block effect: IT share range $-0.36$ to $-1.59$
          across sample cuts; $-0.75$ to $-3.02$ across RECs
    \item Effect driven by the EU-to-ACP direction: consistent with import substitution
          displacing European goods rather than ACP export reorientation
    \item EPAs partially offset the stumbling block: synergy between EPAs and regional
          integration requires simultaneous advancement, not sequential
    \item Policy: EPA conditionality design should account for the trade-diverting phase
          of integration; integration roadmaps should be coordinated with EPA schedules
    \item Limitations: causal identification relies on within-country time variation;
          no exogenous shocks to IT share exploited; future work could use specific tariff
          liberalisation episodes or corridor-infrastructure investments as instruments
  \end{itemize}

\end{enumerate}
